Our Philosophy

Table Football is classic and well enjoyed game, but with unlocked potential. This simple but exciting game introduces features to enhance the enjoyment of Table Football.

You can now customise players and formations, adapt the game difficultly via the pitch tilt, and enjoy the convenience of automatic ball return and scoring!

Get Started!

Four simple additions to make a world of difference

Adaptive Difficulty Mode

Motorized legs allow the tilt of the playing surface to be adjusted. If you are too good for your opponent, you can try playing up-hill!

Customizable Formations

Introduce your own tactics into the game by selecting your favoured formation. Players are removable and interchangeable, allowing for enhanced customization

Ball Freeing Mechanism

The days of lifting the table to free the ball are over. Simply press a button and watch the legs move, jolting the ball back into play

Automatic Scoring & Ball Return

The electronic scoreboard will keep count for you automatically, whilst the ball will return to the spring-launcher via a system of rails.

​ The Tools I used to make my website​

​
​
​
​ The tools I used to build this website are as follows:

1. Bootstrap: I used Bootstrap to find an appropriate template from which I could create my website. By selecting a template with a nice format and appealing style, I was able to focus on the content of my website without needing to write code for the underlying website layout.

This approach is great for saving time and enables you to create a functional and attractive website without prior experience in html coding.



2. Atom: Atom is the text editor I used to create my website. The html code from Bootstrap was copied into Atom and all of the edits that I made to the template in order to create my own website were done in Atom. Saving my file in Atom and refreshing the webpage allowed me to view changes as I made them.



3. GitHub: I used GitHub Desktop in tandem with Atom to keep track of the changes I made to my website. Each time I made a major change, I added a new entry in GitHub with a brief description of the change. This allowed me to keep a record of the changes made, and revert any changes that introduced errors.

Using GitHub and Atom together is a very effective way of preventing bugs from disrupting your website. Even small changes to your code can make a huge difference to the way the website appears, and in GitHub, you can just revert the previous change as soon as you see a bug introduced.

​ Accessibility​

​
​
​
​ In terms of Websites, accessiblility refers to the practise of making sure that no barriers exist to prevent people with disabilities from interacting with or obtaining access to websites. Standard guidelines can be followed to ensure that websites are designed and developed in a way that means that all users (in general) have equal access to information and functionality.

Below are the suggested guidelines for ensuring website accessibility:

1. Alternative Text for Images: All images should include equivalent text (alt text) which briefly describe the photo. This means that people who are visually impaired, can for example, understand the image by using a screen reader that reads alound information on the webpage.

2. Keyboard Input: Some people are unable to use a mouse, so an accessible website must not rely on use of a mouse. All functionality must be available from a keyboard, allowing people with disabilities to use assistive technologies that mimick a keyboard, like speech input for example.

3. Transcripts for Audio: Audio files are unavailable for people that cannot hear, so providing a text transcript of the audio file means that audio information is accessible to people who are deaf or are hard of hearing.

4. Content does not cause seizures: Web contect that flashes at certain rates or in certain patterns can cause seizures or other photosensitive reactions. Therefore, flashing content is ideally avoided completely or implemented in a way that does not cause photosensitive reactions (e.g. by adapting flashing pattern or rate).

​ GDPR​

​
​
​
​ The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy for all individuals within the EU and the European Economic Area. The GDPR contains information and regulation concerning the protection of personal data and how it is used by organisations, and its aim is to give individuals control over their personal data.

The rights of individuals using your website under GDPR are outlined below:


1. Consent: Under GDPR, the conditions for data consent have been strengthed, and companies are no longer able to use long and illegible terms and conditions containing excessive kegalese. The request for consent must be provided in an easily accessible form, and the purpose for personel data processing must be attached to that consent. It must also be as easy to withdraw consent as it is to give it.

2. Breach Notfication: Breach notifications are mandatory where a data breach is likely to "result in a risk for the rights and freedoms of individuals". The breach notification must be generated within 72 hours of first having become aware of a breach. Data processors are required to notify their customers (the controllers) “without undue delay” after first becoming aware of a breach.

3. Right to Access: Individuals have the right to obtain confirmation from the data controller as to whether or not their personal data has been processed, where it has been processed and for what purpose. In fact, the contoller should provide a copy without charge of the personal data, in electronic format.

4. Right to be Forgotten: Individuals have the right to request the data controller to erase their personal data, cease further circulation of the data and (potentially) request third parties cease processing of the personal data.

5. Data Portability: Individuals can receive the personal data concerning them and have the right to trasnfer that data to another controller.

Useful tips for making your website GDPR compliant:

1. Active opt-in forms: Make sure that any forms that invite users to provide contact details for marketing purposes should default to 'no' or be blank.

2. Unbundled opt-in: The consent that you ask for must be set out in seperate questions for accepting terms and conditions, and for accepting the use of data in other ways (e.g. marketing)

3. Make it clear on your website that users have the permisssion to withdraw permission should they want to, and make this an easy process to follow.

4. Ensure your website clearly identifies ALL parties for which consent for which consent is being granted.

​ Marketing​

​
​
​
​

Effective marketing is crucial in encouraging your target market to visit your website. Outlined below are the main marketing steps that I would take to drive traffic to my website:


1. Social Media: Take advantage of social media by advertising my website across a range of different social media platforms

2. Use Search Engine Optimisation (SEO): This considers how search engines work, and algorithms dictate search engine behavior, such as what people search for and keywords that are searched. I would use this internet marketing strategy to optimise my website content and keywords to make it as relevent as possible for those in our target market.

3. Google reviews: I would encourage customers to leave reviews for our product which could be included on google searches.

4. 3rd Party Sites: Include links to my website on all online shopping sites that the product is advertised on. This means that customers who find our product through a 3rd party shopping site will still be encouraged to visit the main site.

5. Email Marketing: Require that customers 'sign up' to purchase something, which if they consent, would allow us to send out marketing emails advertising our product and website.
